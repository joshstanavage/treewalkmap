Japanese Maple cultivar

#5539 | Acer palmatum

Look for: Simple 2-5 inch long/wide with deeply 5- to 7- to 9-lobed, leaves emerge early

Origin: China, Korea, Japan

Japanese Maples are among the most popular small landscape trees. Many, such as this specimen, have a weeping habit and highly dissected purple leaves that enhance their ornamental appeal. In Japan, this species grows as an understory tree beneath the canopy of larger trees, much like our native Flowering Dogwood does in Connecticut.
