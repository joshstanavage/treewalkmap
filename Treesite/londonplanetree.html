London Planetree

Platanus x acerifolia

Look for: Olive-green to creamy bark color

Origin: North American-Asia

Red-brown scales flake to show cream-colored inner bark. Large distinctive leaves with lighter undersides. Fast growing, resistant to anthracnose. Prefers moist, well-drained soil and full sun to light shade. Grows to 75' to 100', 80' spread. (zones 5-9)

This tree is probably more beautiful in winter than in summer, because once the leaves fall off, there is a clearer view of the marvelous multi-colored cream, tan and olive bark. The London Planetree is actually a hybrid species resulting from a cross between the Oriental Planetree and our native American Planetree (Sycamore). It has been used extensively in cities because of its tolerance of pollution and urban conditions.
